# 关于“枪支暴力研究数量”的一点新论据 - 知乎
# 

将近一年前，我写了这篇回答：
[美国控枪为什么这么难？ - Richard Xu 的回答 - 知乎](https://www.zhihu.com/question/39977401/answer/91250383)

> 同时，国会通过了一条法案：拨给CDC用于受伤预防和控制的经费不得用于任何支持或促进控枪的研究。（“None of the funds made available for injury prevention and control at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention may be used to advocate or promote gun control.”）然而，CDC历史上的枪支研究已经告诉我们，**任何研究枪支和枪支暴力犯罪之间关系的研究，都几乎必然会间接地支持或促进控枪。**

CDC的雇员们选择了不要自找麻烦，碰一下枪支研究这条高危黄线，他们将要付出轻至自己的研究经费重至自己的职业生涯的代价。枪支研究不断被边缘化，自20世纪90年代中期到现在，政府支持的枪支致死的研究数量减少了96%。这也使得控枪支持者在和NRA进行控枪辩论时处于下风。

当时依据的材料是两篇新闻报道，恰好，最近（2017年1月3日）发表在JAMA上的一篇Research Letter提供了一个更“实证”的论据：

[Funding and Published Research on Gun Violence and Other Leading Causes of Death](https://link.zhihu.com/?target=http%3A//jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/article-abstract/2595514)

> The United States has the highest rate of gun-related deaths among industrialized countries, with more than 30 000 fatalities annually. To date, research on gun violence has been limited. A 1996 congressional appropriations bill stipulated that “none of the funds made available for injury prevention and control at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention [CDC] may be used to advocate or promote gun control.” Similar restrictions were subsequently extended to other agencies (including the National Institutes of Health), and** although the legislation does not ban gun-related research outright, it has been described as casting a pall over the research community.** This study sought to determine whether funding and publication of gun violence research are disproportionately low relative to the mortality rate from this cause.

摘要中加粗的那句话部分地回应了当时评论中提出的“即使联邦不给钱，私人自己为什么不能出钱做这样的研究”这一观点。

原文的数据结论：

> Gun violence had 1.6% of the funding predicted ($1.4 billion
predicted, $22 million observed) and had 4.5% of the volume
of publications predicted (38 897 predicted, 1738
observed) from the regression analyses.

Reference:

Stark DE, Shah NH. Funding and Publication of Research on Gun Violence and Other Leading Causes of Death. *JAMA.* 2017;317(1):84-85. doi:10.1001/jama.2016.16215
