When a very young child looks at a picture, she can identify simple elements:
 "cat," "book," "chair." Now, computers are getting smart enough to do that too. What's next? In a thrilling talk, computer vision expert Fei-Fei Li describes the state of the art — including the database of 15 million photos her team built to "teach" a computer
 to understand pictures — and the key insights yet to come.
