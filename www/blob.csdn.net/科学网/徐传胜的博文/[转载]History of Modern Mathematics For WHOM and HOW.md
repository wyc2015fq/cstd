**2．TOPICS**

History of Modern Mathematics: For Whom and How.

The occasion of the meeting is to honour Professor Li Wenlin, on his seventieth birthday. To this end we would like to consider the history of mathematics in the early modern and modern periods, from the seventeenth century onward. To address the questions of for whom and how, we plan contributions that examine recent trends in writing history of mathematics. These include, but are not limited to, issues concerning the following themes:

1. Circulation of mathematical knowledge, and the passage from local contexts to large communities;

2. Relative role of textual and non-textual sources in the history of mathematics;

3. Justification and technique in the history of mathematics;

4. Craft and learned practice in the mathematical sciences.

The importance of history of mathematics for mathematicians and students of mathematics will be underlined in the presentations and discussions. At the same time, the renewed importance of the history of mathematics to philosophers investigating the philosophy of mathematical practice will be highlighted in some papers.
