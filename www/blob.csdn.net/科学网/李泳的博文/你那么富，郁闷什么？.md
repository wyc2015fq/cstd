# 你那么富，郁闷什么？

前夜元宵无月，随便翻开一本书，是Dickens的圣诞故事，却见他生于1812年2月7日——真巧啊，200岁的生日。后来才见Google换了涂鸦——晚了半天。



D老最有名的圣诞故事是***A Christmas Carol***（《圣诞颂歌》）。它除了那个已经成为普通名词的角色Scrooge——老D的形容很妙：hard and sharp as flint, from which no steel had ever struck out generous fire：心如铁石，却磨不出一点儿慈悲的火星（开篇几段对Scrooge的描述，有趣极了，大家自己去看吧）——还留下了很多“名言”。第一个对话就很有意思——



‘**A merry Christmas**, uncle! God save you!’ cried a cheerful voice. It was the voice of Scrooge's nephew, who came upon him so quickly that this was the first intimation he had of his approach. 

**`Bah!' said Scrooge, `Humbug!' **

He had so heated himself with rapid walking in the fog and frost, this nephew of Scrooge's, that he was all in a glow; his face was ruddy and handsome; his eyes sparkled, and his breath smoked again. 

‘**Christmas a humbug**, uncle!' said Scrooge's nephew. `You don't mean that, I am sure?’

‘I do,’said Scrooge. ‘Merry Christmas! **What right have you to be merry? What reason have you to be merry? You're poor enough**.’

‘Come, then,’ returned the nephew gaily. ‘**What right have you to be dismal? What reason have you to be morose? You're rich enough**.’

Scrooge having no better answer ready on the spur of the moment, **said ****‘****Bah!****’**** again; and followed it up with ****‘****Humbug.**’





Merry Christmas，这可能是世界上第一声标准的圣诞问候，而Scrooge的回答，Bah Humbug，大概也是最有趣的。



当然，最现实主义的问答还是



**——你那么穷，有什么可乐的？**

**——你那么富，又郁闷什么？**
