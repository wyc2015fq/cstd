# 白宫的13把钥匙                                                   ![](./static/image/blog/recommendico.gif)精选

华盛顿American University的政治史家Allan Lichtman在1990年写过一本*The Thirteen Keys To The Presidency* (Lanham: Madison Books, 1990l) ，然后修订为The Keys To The White House (1996)，今年直接改为更唬人的Predicting the Next President: The Keys to the White House（Rowman& Littlefield Publishers, 2016）——他根据1860-1980年的美国大选历史建立了一个“预言系统”，并成功预测了1984-2012（两个有趣的年份）的大选结果。现在他预测老普会赢。

 Allan的13把钥匙是
- 
Party     Mandate: After the midterm elections, the incumbent party holds more seats     in the U.S. House of Representatives than after the previous midterm     elections.

- 
Contest:     There is no serious contest for the incumbent party nomination.

- 
Incumbency:     The incumbent party candidate is the sitting president.

- 
Third party:     There is no significant third party or independent campaign.

- 
Short-term     economy: The economy is not in recession during the election campaign.

- 
Long-term     economy: Real per capita economic growth during the term equals or exceeds     mean growth during the previous two terms.

- 
Policy     change: The incumbent administration effects major changes in national     policy.

- 
Social     unrest: There is no sustained social unrest during the term.

- 
Scandal: The     incumbent administration is untainted by major scandal.

- 
Foreign/military     failure: The incumbent administration suffers no major failure in foreign     or military affairs.

- 
Foreign/military     success: The incumbent administration achieves a major success in foreign     or military affairs.

- 
Incumbent     charisma: The incumbent party candidate is charismatic or a national hero.

- 
Challenger     charisma: The challenging party candidate is not charismatic or a national     hero.


这是13个是考验执政党的true/false问题。Allan的判据是，如果答案的F超过6个，他（她）就输了。现在看来。民主党在五点（1, 3, 7, 11和12 ）已经F了，再多一个F，希太就输定了。

在Allan的体系中，假如第三方竞选者获得5%以上的支持，第4点也F了。现在Gary Johnson都过10%了。所以，Allan预测老普会赢。不过，考虑到老普的史无前例（given the unprecedented nature of the Trump candidacy and Trump himself），他也可能“逆流而输”（ he could defy all odds and lose even though the verdict of history is in his favor），所以结果还是两分的（So this would also suggest, you know, the possibility this election could go either way）——他自己的这句话就足够把他的理论扔进希太的basket of deplorablse。

Allan的逻辑很简单：如果当政的一方表现不好，他们的候选人就会输。这种预测，即使每回都对了，也没有任何模型意义。何况,相同的逻辑也可以用于在野党的候选者——所以华盛顿邮报要强调，No, we are not making this endorsement simply because Ms. Clinton's opponent is dreadful.

不过，他们似乎都没考虑到“意外事件”的影响。意外的影响，就像蝴蝶的翅膀，有时很温柔，有时却会让暴风雨来的更猛烈——些吧。
