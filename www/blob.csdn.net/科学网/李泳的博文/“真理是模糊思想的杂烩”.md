在Amazon找书，看到精装本的***The Story of Art***降价了，比平装本还便宜，于是订了一本——十多年前从香港带回一个平装本，也是第16版。此书1950年初版，1995年第16版，每年都在重印——顺便搭一本赠品，是**Sherwood Anderson**的《俄亥俄温斯堡》（***Winesburg, Ohio***）。我以前不知道其人其书，选它只是因为选不出其他了。没想到这位“老舍”也是大家，广告说他是海明威心目中的大师。



《不列颠百科》对他的介绍是



who strongly influenced American writing between World Wars I and II, particularly the technique of the short story. His writing had an impact on such notable writers as **Ernest [](ebcid:com.britannica.oec2.identifier.IndexEntryContentIdentifier?idxStructId=260825&library=EB)[Hemingway](ebcid:com.britannica.oec2.identifier.ArticleIdentifier?articleId=39962&library=EB&query=null&title=Hemingway#9039962.toc)**and **William [](ebcid:com.britannica.oec2.identifier.IndexEntryContentIdentifier?idxStructId=202695&library=EB)[Faulkner](ebcid:com.britannica.oec2.identifier.ArticleIdentifier?articleId=33829&library=EB&query=null&title=Faulkner#9033829.toc)**, both of whom owe the first publication of their books to his efforts. His prose style, based on everyday speech and derived from the experimental writing of **Gertrude Stein**, was markedly influential on the early Hemingway—who parodied it cruelly in *Torrents of Spring* (1926) to make a clean break and become his own man. (Encyclopædia Britannica**.*****Encyclopædia Britannica Ultimate Reference Suite****.* Chicago:  Encyclopædia Britannica, 2011.)



其中提到的**Gertrude Stein**或许名气更大，她跟**Henry James**学心理学，后来移居法国，与兄弟收藏当代画家的画，毕加索为她画过肖像。她住在巴黎，她的家是沙龙，海外美国作家——“垮掉的一代”（**Lost Generation**）就是她给起的名字——在那儿聚会，而海明威和这位老舍先生就在其中。她说过一句很有气魄的话：**Einstein was the creative philosophic mind of the century, and I have been the creative literary mind of the century.**



老舍的这本小书，描写了俄亥俄小城Winesburg的怪异（**Grotesque**）生活。有中译本叫《小城畸人》，不知是否恰当——庄子说，“畸人者，畸于人而侔于天。”“侔”就是“齐”，与“畸”相对。可见，“畸人”是特异之人，未必等于“怪诞之人”。



作者假装说见过一个怪老头儿写的“怪异故事”（***The Book of Grotesque***——**Edgar Allan Poe**早就写过系列***Tales of the Grotesque and Arabesque***，二者大概是不同的），留下了很深的印象，凭它理解了很多人和事。他总结那书的中心思想是：世界年轻时有很多思想，但没有真理。真理是人自己造的，每个真理都是很多模糊思想的杂烩（**each truth was a composite of a great many vague thoughts**）。



老头儿认为，真理会令人变得古怪。如果谁把一个真理据为己有，照着它去生活，那真理也就成了谬误。（**the moment one of the people took one of the truths to himself, called it his truth, and tried to live his life by it, he became a grotesque and the truth he embraced became a falsehood.**）
