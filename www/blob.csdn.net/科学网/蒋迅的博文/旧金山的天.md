# 旧金山的天

这个图片是一个视频截图，上面有360个小的图片。每一个小图片代表着某一天的天空。旧金山探索馆在房顶上放了一个对着天空的照相机，从日出到日落每十秒拍一张照片，然后集结在一起制成视频。所以这张图片就代表着旧金山在360天里下午2点48分时的天空。天空真蓝啊。下面是NASA的英文 [原文](http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap130724.html)：

Each panel shows one day. With 360 movie panels, the sky over (almost) an entire year is shown in time lapse format [as recorded](http://www.murphlab.com/hsky/) by a video camera on the roof of the [Exploratorium](http://www.exploratorium.edu/) museum in [San Francisco](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Francisco), [California](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California). The camera recorded an image every 10 seconds from before [sunrise](http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap120920.html) to after [sunset](http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap120522.html) and from mid-2009 to mid-2010. A time stamp showing the local time of day is provided on the lower right. [The videos](http://vimeo.com/32095756) are arranged chronologically, with July 28 shown on the upper left, and January 1 located about about half way down. Although every day lasts 24 hours, [daylight lasts longest](http://answers.yahoo.com/question/index?qid=20130118171218AAbk8TE) in the northern hemisphere in June and the surrounding summer months, a fact which can be seen here as the bottom (and soon top) videos are the first to light up with dawn. The initial darkness in the middle depicts the delayed dawn and fewer daylight hours of winter. In [the videos](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PNln_me-XjI&f), darkness indicates night, blue depicts clear day, while gray portrays pervasive daytime cloud cover. Many videos show [complex patterns of clouds moving](http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap110516.html) across the camera's wide field as that day progresses. As the videos collectively end, [sunset](http://apod.nasa.gov/apod/ap120520.html) and then darkness descend first on the winter days just above the middle, and last on the mid-summer near the bottom
